CES2018 -- Provides a pre-2020 benchmark of Chinese-American partisan alignment and vote choice shortly after the 2016 election. Used to identify early signs of Republican gains within the Chinese-American population.
CES2020 -- Measures party identification and vote choice during the 2020 election cycle;  Enables comparison with 2018 to track directional trends.
CES2022 -- Serves as the post-2016 trend endpoint for partisan realignment. Shows whether any shift toward the Republican Party persisted into the midterm elections. 
PEW22-23 -- Includes detailed Chinese ethnic identifiers and multiple immigration attitude variables, allowing for direct measurement of whether more restrictive immigration views increase the probability of Republican alignment among Chinese-American voters.
FBI Crime 2018 to 2025 (Texas)  -- The Crime rates in Texas from 2018 to 2025. It can be used to examine if crime rates affect how Chinese Americans vote. Picked out states that have an outsized Chinese American population. 
FBI 2018 to 2025 (NYC) -- The Crime rates in New York City from 2018 to 2025. It can be used to examine if crime rates affect how Chinese Americans vote. Picked out states that have an outsized Chinese American population. 
FBI 2018 to 2025 (Cali) -- The Crime rates in California from 2018 to 2025. It can be used to examine if crime rates affect how Chinese Americans vote. Picked out states that have an outsized Chinese American population. 
MIT Election Data and Science Lab -- County Presidential Election Returns from 2000 to 2024. This is to look at the results of the states that have an outsized Chinese American population and see if they voted Republican due to the factors in our hypothesis. 
US Census ACS -- The population of Chinese Americans across each state. This is to decide on counties with sizable Chinese-American populations  to keep analysis to focus on relevant areas.
